Plays That Win in FSI.

Four sales motions mapped to FSI Singapore scenarios. Identify the primary motion at the start of each deal cycle to tailor messaging and competitive positioning.

Replace

Competitive Takeout — Replace Incumbent Vendor

FSI Scenario

  • Bank is frustrated with Datadog/Splunk/Dynatrace costs scaling with telemetry growth
  • Unpredictable billing from proprietary per-host or per-container pricing
  • Vendor lock-in with proprietary agents and query languages
  • Contract renewal window opening in next 6 months

How to Win

  • Lead with competitive differentiation — they already know they have a problem
  • Quantify TCO savings: usage-based pricing vs. per-host/per-container
  • Emphasize open standards (OTel) = no lock-in, even with Grafana
  • Highlight Adaptive Telemetry for 50-80% cost reduction on noisy data
  • Reference: Erste Digital, Blockchain company migrated from Datadog
Migrate

OSS to Cloud — Upgrade Self-Managed Grafana

FSI Scenario

  • Bank runs 50+ self-hosted Grafana instances with Prometheus backends
  • SRE team spending 30-40% of time maintaining observability stack
  • Scaling issues, security patching burden, compliance gaps
  • No RBAC, audit logging, or enterprise features

How to Win

  • Quantify engineering time & cost of self-hosting
  • Emphasize compliance: automatic upgrades, security patches, audit logging
  • MAS TRM compliance made easier with managed service + RBAC
  • Reference: Uni Cards — from OSS to Cloud, freed engineering time
  • BYOC option for accounts spending $5M+ who need data in their own cloud
Select

First Pane of Glass — Unified Frontend Land

FSI Scenario

  • FSI has fragmented tools: CloudWatch, Splunk for logs, New Relic for APM, Prometheus for infra
  • No unified view — teams context-switch between 4-5 tools during incidents
  • Large-scale cloud migration needs real-time visibility dashboard
  • New digital bank selecting observability platform for the first time

How to Win

  • Position Grafana as the "single pane of glass" across all data sources
  • Low-risk entry: connect to existing tools first, then migrate backends
  • Educate first, differentiate second (especially for greenfield)
  • Use big tent data source integrations as the wedge
  • Reference: Erste Digital — consolidated 250+ instances into unified Cloud

Competitive Intelligence

Know Your Competition.

Primary competitors in Singapore FSI accounts. For each, know their weaknesses and how to counter-position Grafana.

Datadog

Most Common Incumbent

Their Weaknesses

  • Cost at scale: Per-host pricing explodes with K8s/microservices growth
  • Vendor lock-in: Proprietary agent, DD-native query language, no OTel-first
  • Unpredictable billing: SKU complexity, container surcharges, overage fees
  • Data retention costs: Long-term log retention extremely expensive
  • Limited customization: "Walled garden" — less composable than Grafana

Our Counter-Positioning

  • Usage-based pricing: Pay for what you use, not per host/container
  • Open standards: OTel-native, no proprietary agents — switch cost = zero
  • Adaptive Telemetry: Reduce noisy data by up to 80% while keeping fidelity
  • Big Tent: Grafana connects to Datadog as a data source during phased migration
  • Proof: Financial infra company saved significantly migrating from DD to Grafana Cloud

Splunk

Log Analytics Incumbent

Their Weaknesses

  • Extremely expensive logs: GB-based pricing makes full-fidelity retention prohibitive
  • Siloed from metrics/traces: Bolt-on APM, not unified observability
  • Complex licensing: Workload pricing adds confusion
  • Heavy on-prem footprint: Many SG banks still running on-prem Splunk
  • Cisco acquisition uncertainty: Product direction questions post-acquisition

Our Counter-Positioning

  • Loki: $0.50/GB logs with built-in retention — fraction of Splunk cost
  • Unified LGTM stack: Metrics, logs, traces, profiles correlated natively
  • Grafana as first pane: Connect to Splunk as data source during migration
  • Open future: No acquisition risk — independent, VC-backed, $6B+ valuation
  • Adaptive Logs: Auto-reduce volume while preserving query capability

Dynatrace

Enterprise APM

Their Weaknesses

  • Per-host pricing: Expensive at scale — 8GB host units add up fast
  • Proprietary OneAgent: Deep lock-in — removal is painful and risky
  • Opaque AI: "Davis AI" = black box — regulated FSI needs transparency
  • Weak community: Small ecosystem vs. Grafana's 25M+ user community
  • Limited composability: Tightly coupled stack, poor multi-tool integration

Our Counter-Positioning

  • Transparent AI: Grafana Assistant shows its logic and data sources — auditable
  • Open instrumentation: OTel SDKs replace proprietary agents
  • Knowledge Graph: Automated topology discovery without proprietary agents
  • Community-driven: 25M+ users, 1M+ dashboards — proven at scale
  • Cost-effective APM: App Observability + Profiles at fraction of DT cost

New Relic

Mid-Market APM

Their Weaknesses

  • Per-user pricing: Full-platform user pricing limits observability democratization
  • Data ingest caps: Free tier limits create "data diets" — running blind
  • Reliability concerns: Public outages eroded trust in 2023-2024
  • Weaker in infrastructure: Historically APM-focused, infra monitoring is secondary
  • Financial stability: Ongoing profitability questions vs. Grafana's growth trajectory

Our Counter-Positioning

  • Unlimited users: Grafana Cloud viewers are free — democratize observability
  • No data diets: Usage-based pricing means you send what you need
  • Full-stack native: Metrics, logs, traces, profiles, k8s, synthetics — all built together
  • Proven reliability: 7 of top 10 Fortune 10 companies trust Grafana
  • Flexible Spend Commit: Pre-loaded funds across any product, no expiry

Four positioning pillars tailored for Singapore Financial Services accounts.

MAS encourages technology resilience and avoiding concentration risk. Grafana's OpenTelemetry-native approach means FSIs are never locked into a single vendor. Data portability and open standards align with MAS TRM guidelines on vendor risk management.

Predictable Cost at Scale

Compliance-Ready Architecture

AI-Powered Incident Response
