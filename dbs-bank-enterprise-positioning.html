DBS Bank is Southeast Asia's largest bank by assets and has been named the "World's Best Digital Bank" multiple times. 💎 Grafana Enterprise — Feature-by-Feature Value Map for Banking

1. Security & Access Control

Critical for MAS Compliance

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Fine-grained permissions at the dashboard, folder, data source, and team level. Critical for a bank with thousands of engineers where you need to ensure trading floor dashboards aren't visible to retail banking teams, and vice versa.

Data Source Permissions

Control which teams can query which backends. In a bank, this is essential — production database metrics should only be accessible to authorized SREs, not every developer.

Enhanced Authentication (SAML, OAuth 2.0, LDAP)

Deep integration with enterprise identity providers. DBS likely uses a sophisticated IdP — Enterprise provides Team Sync to auto-map IdP groups to Grafana teams and roles.

2. Audit & Compliance

Regulatory Requirement

Audit Logging

Every action in Grafana is logged — who viewed what dashboard, who changed what alert, who accessed what data source. For MAS TRM compliance, this isn't optional. OSS doesn't have this.

Usage Insights / Analytics

Understand who is using Grafana, which dashboards are most viewed, which are stale. Helps platform teams make data-driven decisions about which dashboards to invest in and which to deprecate.

Reporting & Scheduled Exports

Generate PDF/CSV reports on schedule and email them to stakeholders. Regulators, CTO, risk officers — they want reports, not dashboard links.

4. Enterprise Support & Partnership

Operational De-Risk

For a systemically important bank, the risk of running unsupported OSS in production is significant:

  • 24/7 Enterprise Support with SLAs — when Grafana goes down at 3am during a market event, they need someone to call
  • Priority Security Patches — vulnerabilities get patched faster, and DBS gets notified proactively
  • Upgrade Assistance — Enterprise customers get help with major version upgrades (which can be complex at scale)
  • Direct Roadmap Influence — as Informatica said: "We want to give feedback about the product back to the Grafana team... those conversations have been very constructive"

🚀 Recommended Sales Motion

Phase 1: Discovery & Validation (Weeks 1-2)

  1. Map the current deployment: How many Grafana instances? How many users? Which data sources? What's their upgrade/patch cadence?
  2. Identify the "builder tax": What custom tooling have they built around Grafana? (SSO integration, audit logging, RBAC middleware, custom data source connectors)
  3. Understand regulatory pressure: What MAS TRM requirements apply to their observability tooling? Is running unsupported OSS a risk finding?
  4. Find the champion: Platform engineering leads who feel the pain of maintaining the infrastructure layer

Phase 2: Value Demonstration (Weeks 3-4)

  1. Run a "Builder Tax" workshop: Map the engineering effort going into undifferentiated Grafana maintenance vs. banking feature development
  2. Enterprise plugin demo: Show connectivity to their specific backend systems (Oracle, ServiceNow, etc.)
  3. RBAC & audit demo: Show how Enterprise governance features replace their custom-built solutions
  4. Reference calls: Connect DBS with Informatica or Siemens — builder-culture companies that chose Enterprise

Phase 3: Business Case & Close (Weeks 5-8)

  1. Quantify the builder tax: X engineers × Y hours/month on Grafana maintenance = $Z opportunity cost
  2. Risk mitigation: Frame Enterprise support as regulatory risk reduction (audit trail, security patches, SLAs)
  3. Phased rollout: Propose starting with their most critical cluster/team — prove value, then expand
  4. Multi-year deal: Offer better pricing on 2-3 year commitment to match their long-term platform strategy

⚠️ Objection Handling

ObjectionResponse
"We can build what we need ourselves" Absolutely — and you already have. The question is whether building audit logging, RBAC, and data source connectors is the highest-value use of your platform engineers' time. Every sprint on infra maintenance is a sprint not spent on banking innovation.
"OSS is working fine for us" It is — for now. But as you scale to more teams and more dashboards, governance becomes exponentially harder. Erste Digital had 250 OSS instances before consolidating. At what scale does your current approach break?
"We don't want vendor lock-in" Enterprise IS the same open source Grafana — same codebase, same API, same dashboards. Enterprise features are additive. If you ever stop the subscription, you still have fully functional Grafana OSS. Zero lock-in.
"Cloud is not an option for us" That's exactly what Enterprise is designed for. Self-managed, on your infrastructure, behind your firewall, under your control. No data leaves your environment.
"The price is too high" Let's calculate the cost of your current approach: engineering time maintaining custom RBAC + audit + SSO + data connectors + the risk cost of no SLA on your observability platform. Enterprise is almost always cheaper than the builder tax.
"We need to evaluate alternatives" You've already chosen Grafana — your teams use it, your dashboards are built, your runbooks reference it. Enterprise isn't a migration; it's a license key. The switching cost to an alternative is orders of magnitude higher.

💡 Discovery Questions to Ask DBS

  1. "How many Grafana instances are you currently running, and how do you manage consistency across them?"
  2. "Walk me through what happens when a new team member needs Grafana access — how long does provisioning take?"
  3. "How do you currently handle audit trails for dashboard access? Has this come up in MAS TRM reviews?"
  4. "If your Grafana deployment has a critical issue at 2am during a market event, what's your escalation path today?"
  5. "How much engineering time per month goes into maintaining your Grafana infrastructure versus building banking features?"
  6. "Which enterprise systems (Oracle, ServiceNow, etc.) would you want to visualize in Grafana if you could connect them natively?"
  7. "How do you manage data source access — can every engineer query every backend, or do you need isolation?"
  8. "What's your current Grafana version, and how do you handle major version upgrades across your fleet?"
  9. "Do your compliance or risk teams have specific requirements around the tools your SREs use in production?"
  10. "If we could give your platform team back 20% of their time currently spent on Grafana maintenance, what would they build?"

🔮 Future-State Vision: Plant the Seed for Expansion

While DBS isn't ready for Cloud today, Enterprise creates the relationship and trust foundation for future expansion:

  • Grafana Enterprise → BYOC (Bring Your Own Cloud): If DBS moves workloads to AWS/GCP, BYOC gives them Grafana Cloud capabilities running in their cloud account — full data sovereignty

Land & Expand: Enterprise is the wedge. Start with the platform team's most critical cluster, prove the value of governance + support, then expand across the bank.
